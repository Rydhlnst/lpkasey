About  Te  Pae  O  Te  Rangi  
Te  Pae  O  Te  Rangi  is  a  place  of  healing,  connection,  growth  and  empowerment  grounded  in  kaupapa  
Māori
 
values.
 
We  believe  in  creating  safe  spaces  where  people  can  come  together  to  heal,  learn,  grow,  reconnect  
and
 
strengthen
 
their
 
sense
 
of
 
identity,
 
purpose
 
and
 
wellbeing.

ABOUT  PAGE  
The  Meaning  Behind  Te  Pae  O  Te  Rangi  
Te  Pae  O  Te  Rangi  metaphorically  means  the  meeting  place  for  ALL  to  come.  
The  meeting  of  the  land  and  the  sky.  
The  silver  lining  which  we  would  call  Tau  or  the  place  where  UJ  and  LJ  meet.  
A  place  where  everyone  can  meet  to  be  vulnerable  but  most  importantly  safe.

Te  Whiringawhā

of
 
strong
 
organisation
 
and
 
positive
 
engagement.

outstanding,
 
inclusive,
 
long
 
term

TE  WHIRINGAWHĀ  is  a  matrix  that  explores:  
●  The  powershare  dynamics  of  Te  Ao  Māori  
 ●  How  traditional  Māori  contexts  can  be  organised

●  The  Māori  psyche  into  four  requisite  modalities  
 
TE  WHIRINGAWHĀ  is:

 ●  A  lens  providing  insight  and  richer  context  about  the  traditional  management  styles  present

Leadership  &  Identity  
Te  Pae  O  Te  Rangi  Rangatahi  Hiking  Leadership  
Initiative
 
Designed  to  empower  rangatahi  aged  12–24  through  experiential  learning  in  a  natural  outdoor  
environment.
 
The  initiative  focuses  on  essential  life  skills  such  as  water  purification,  emotional  wellness,  fire  lighting  
and
 
shelter
 
building
 
while
 
promoting
 
teamwork
 
and
 
leadership
 
development.
 
By  engaging  with  Māori  leadership  archetypes  —  Leader,  Learner,  Leverager  and  Lover  —  
participants
 
strengthen
 
their
 
identities
 
and
 
connection
 
to
 
their
 
cultural
 
heritage.
 
 
Mau  Rākau  Programme  
Mau  Rākau  is  a  journey  of  personal  and  cultural  growth.  
Through  self-management,  communication  and  relationship-building,  taiohi  connect  with  their  
whakapapa,
 
hone
 
their
 
skills
 
and
 
strengthen
 
their
 
wairua,
 
hinengaro
 
and
 
tinana.
 
Taiohi  will:  
●  Learn  the  origins  of  the  Taiaha  
 ●  Understand  marae  tikanga  
 ●  Push  through  challenges  
 ●  Honour  the  tapu,  mauri  and  mana  of  each  other  and  the  kaupapa  
 
Together,  we  aim  to  build  confident  taiohi  who  can:  
●  Lead  with  AROHA  
 ●  Learn  with  RESPECT  
 ●  Support  with  UNITY

Healing  &  Wellbeing  
Healing  Through  Music  Programme  –  Te  Manu  Puoro  
Te  Manu  Puoro  uses  the  creative  process  of  healing  and  music  in  order  to  help  the  individual  or  group  
explore
 
and
 
strengthen
 
their
 
inner
 
feelings.
 
This  helps  stimulate  self-awareness,  build  resilience  and  improve  self-esteem.  
People  come  together  to  be  led,  to  learn  information,  to  show  concern  and  to  celebrate.  
They  are  united  by  their  causes,  their  culture,  their  circumstances  and  the  common  good.  
Music  is  universal.  
Music  is  personal.  
Music  is  art.  
Music  is  healing.  
 
Journaling  Programme  –  Te  Manu  Rongoā  O  Te  Rangi  
Te  Manu  Rongoā  O  Te  Rangi  uses  the  creative  process  of  healing  and  journaling  in  order  to  help  the  
individual
 
or
 
group
 
explore
 
and
 
strengthen
 
their
 
inner
 
feelings.
 
Journaling  is  a  profound  act  of  self-reflection  and  self-expression.  
It  allows  you  to  release  the  burden  of  holding  onto  emotions  and  experiences,  leading  to  healing  and  
a
 
greater
 
understanding
 
of
 
yourself.
 
 
Romiromi  /  Mirimiri  Sessions  
Whether  you  need  release,  reset  or  realignment  —  this  is  deep,  intuitive  bodywork  grounded  in  
kaupapa
 
Māori
 
healing.
 
Pressure  is  applied  to  the  haemata  (accumulation  points)  on  the  body,  which  may  involve  working  
directly
 
on
 
the
 
table
 
using
 
body
 
weight
 
or
 
addressing
 
areas
 
like
 
the
 
groin,
 
and
 
occasionally
 
incorporating
 
rākau
 
or
 
kohatu
 
for
 
further
 
healing
 
support.

It  is  important  that  you  breathe  well  throughout  the  process.  
Releasing  the  breath  openly  through  the  mouth  during  the  process  will  aid  your  body  in  releasing  what  
no
 
longer
 
serves
 
it.
